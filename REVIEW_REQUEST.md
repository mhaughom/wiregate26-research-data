# Independent scientific and editorial review requested

Agreement with the cable-contact hypothesis is not requested. Reviewers are
asked to identify errors, unsupported certainty and plausible alternatives.

## Tracking and physics questions

1. Do the nine recorded ATS segments regenerate the incident CSV exactly?
2. Are coordinate, timing and flight-window choices reproducible and justified?
3. Is the discrete impulse-time search implemented and interpreted correctly?
4. Are models A–D adequate comparators, and what omitted alternatives matter?
5. Is the three-metre continuation a fair like-for-like touchdown comparison?
6. How should uncertainty be estimated without source covariance or filtering metadata?
7. Can tracking artefact, latency, wind, spin evolution or another interaction explain the change?

## Editorial and rights questions

1. Are recorded facts, calculations and illustrative cable geometry clearly separated?
2. Is the FIFA/BBC/Immersiv provenance stated accurately without implying endorsement?
3. Are the data notice and lack of an asserted open-data licence sufficiently prominent?
4. Does any wording claim cable contact more strongly than the public coordinates support?

Open a scientific-review issue with the release or commit reviewed, expertise,
method, findings and any permission to name the reviewer. A project self-review
is not an independent review.
